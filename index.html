Lunar Lander Offline RL

Investigating the impact of demonstration data quality on Decision Transformer

View Detailed Experiment Summary

Abstract

This experiment evaluates how the quality of demonstration data affects Offline Reinforcement Learning agents, comparing the MLP-based Conservative Q-Learning (CQL) and the Sequence-based Decision Transformer (DT) algorithms. Unlike standard Reinforcement Learning which learns by trial and error, Offline RL learns exclusively from a fixed dataset. We compared four data levels: Expert (fully trained), Intermediate (partially proficient), Medium (partially trained), and Random (untrained).

Conclusion

The results provide a clear demonstration of the architectural trade-offs between Decision Transformers (DT) and Conservative Q-Learning (CQL) across varying data qualities:

  • Sequence Modeling Advantage: On higher quality data (Expert and Intermediate), DT consistently tracks or exceeds the teacher's performance. At the Intermediate level (105.5 mean), DT managed to double the success rate of CQL (4% vs 2%), showing a superior ability to mimic the successful trajectories buried in sub-optimal data.
  • Robustness of Q-Learning on Low-Quality Data: On Medium and Random data, CQL remains significantly more robust. In the Random dataset, CQL successfully "stitched" noisy actions to improve over the dataset mean, whereas DT collapsed into erratic behavior due to its reliance on whole-trajectory patterns that didn't exist.

Ultimately, Decision Transformers are powerful imitators when provided with some successful examples, but value-based methods like CQL are better at extracting signal from pure noise.

Appendix: Experiment Setup

1. Environment

The experiment was conducted using Gymnasium's LunarLander-v3. This is a classic "box2d" control task where the agent must land a craft on a designated pad using three engines (Main, Left, Right).

2. Dataset Generation

Four distinct datasets were generated using Stable-Baselines3 (PPO), each containing 500,000 transitions. The levels are defined by the specific training state of the teacher agent:

  • Expert (Threshold: ≥ 200.0): The PPO agent was trained to full convergence. It demonstrates optimal control, consistently landing fuel-efficiently on the center of the pad.
  • Intermediate (Threshold: ≈ 100.0): The PPO agent was trained until its rolling average reward reached exactly 100.0. It has mastered flight stability and descent, consistently reaching the surface upright, but often lacks the precision to hit the landing pad or control final impact velocity perfectly.
  • Medium (Threshold: ≈ 20.0): The PPO agent was trained until its rolling average reward reached exactly 20.0. It can control its orientation and initiate a descent (avoiding immediate crashes), but it mostly hovers or lands far from the target area.
  • Random (N/A): Actions are sampled uniformly from the discrete action space. It represents a "zero-knowledge" baseline with no coherent flight behavior.

3. Training Details

The offline agents were trained using the Conservative Q-Learning (CQL) and Decision Transformer (DT) implementation from the d3rlpy library.

  • Steps: 30,000 gradient steps per model.
  • Architecture: DiscreteDecisionTransformer (context_size=50 or 20, max_timestep=1000) vs DiscreteCQLConfig (MLP default).
  • Hardware: Training performed on GPU (NVIDIA RTX 2080 via CUDA 12.1).
