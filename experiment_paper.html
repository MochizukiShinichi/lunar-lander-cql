Lunar Lander Offline RL: Experimentation History

A commit-by-commit analysis of the project's experimental evolution.

Introduction

This document provides a detailed, commit-based historical analysis of the experimentation conducted for the Lunar Lander Offline Reinforcement Learning project. Each section corresponds to a significant commit, detailing its experimental purpose, the approach taken, key metrics considered, and the impact on the project's understanding of Offline RL algorithms.

Commit Analysis

Commit 5f0a70e: Initial commit: Lunar Lander Offline RL Data Comparison Report

This commit laid the groundwork for the entire experimental framework, focusing initially on establishing a baseline comparison using the Conservative Q-Learning (CQL) algorithm.

Experimentation History & Purpose:

  • To set up the foundational experimental pipeline for evaluating Offline Reinforcement Learning performance based on demonstration data quality.
  • Initial focus on understanding CQL's behavior across different data qualities (Expert, Medium, Random).

Approach:

  • Data Generation: Utilized PPO from stable_baselines3 to create three distinct datasets (Expert, Medium, Random) for the LunarLander-v3 environment. Each dataset comprised 50,000 transitions.
  • Model Training: Trained DiscreteCQLConfig models using the d3rlpy library on each of the generated datasets for 30,000 gradient steps.
  • Evaluation & Visualization: Developed scripts to:
    • Evaluate trained CQL models over 50 test episodes.
    • Compare dataset mean rewards against trained model mean rewards.
    • Generate bar charts (performance_comparison.png) and simulation videos for visual analysis.

Metrics:

  • Mean Cumulative Reward (for both datasets and trained models).
  • Success Rate (episodes with reward ≥ 200).
  • Crash Rate (heuristic for episodes with reward ≤ -100).

Key Files:

  • generate_data.py: For dataset creation.
  • train_cql.py: For training CQL models.
  • evaluate_and_visualize.py: For model evaluation and result presentation.
  • index.html: Initial web report for CQL results.

Commit 9a0fca0: Integrate Decision Transformer with full GPU training and side-by-side rendering

This commit marked a significant expansion of the experimental scope by introducing the Decision Transformer (DT) algorithm, allowing for a direct comparative analysis with CQL.

Experimentation History & Purpose:

  • To integrate a state-of-the-art sequence-modeling Offline RL algorithm (Decision Transformer) into the comparison framework.
  • To compare the performance characteristics of DT against CQL, particularly concerning their sensitivities to data quality.

Approach:

  • New Algorithm: Incorporated DiscreteDecisionTransformerConfig from d3rlpy.
  • Enhanced Data Generation: Increased dataset size for Expert, Medium, and Random from 50,000 to 500,000 transitions, benefiting data-hungry Transformer models.
  • GPU Training: Configured DT model training to leverage GPU (CUDA) for accelerated computation, recognizing the computational demands of Transformer architectures.
  • Comparative Evaluation: Modified evaluation scripts to:
    • Handle both CQL and DT models.
    • Utilize StatefulTransformerWrapper with a fixed target_return=200.0 for DT evaluation.
    • Update result tables and bar charts to display comparative metrics for both algorithms.
    • Record side-by-side simulation videos for CQL and DT agents.
  • New Utility Scripts: Added minimal_example.py for quick sanity checks and test_dt.py/test_video.py for DT-specific testing.

Metrics:

  • Direct comparison of CQL Mean Reward vs. DT Mean Reward.
  • Direct comparison of CQL Success Rate (%) vs. DT Success Rate (%).
  • The fixed target_return of 200.0 for DT evaluation served as a key conditioning parameter.

Key Files:

  • Modified: evaluate_and_visualize.py, generate_data.py, index.html (major updates).
  • Introduced: dt_expert.d3, dt_medium.d3, dt_random.d3 (model files); minimal_example.py, test_dt.py, test_video.py.

Commit 440368c: Update report to reflect true medium model results across datasets

This commit addressed inaccuracies in the evaluation of models trained on sub-optimal (specifically "medium") data, leading to a more robust and realistic assessment of Decision Transformer performance.

Experimentation History & Purpose:

  • To refine the definition and generation of the "medium" dataset to be more precisely controlled.
  • To re-evaluate Decision Transformer performance with a more appropriate target_return, recognizing the limitations of asking a DT to extrapolate far beyond its training data's quality.

Approach:

  • "True Medium" Dataset: Introduced train_true_medium.py to generate a medium dataset where the PPO agent was explicitly trained to a `reward_threshold=20.0`, ensuring a consistent and well-defined intermediate performance level.
  • Dynamic target_return: Critically, the DT evaluation in evaluate_and_visualize.py was updated to set the `target_return` dynamically based on the dataset's quality:
    • Expert: 200.0
    • Medium: 40.0 (a realistic achievable return for a medium agent).
    • Random: -100.0 (a low, but realistic target for random data).
    This change ensured that DT was being queried with achievable goals for its given training data.
  • Recalculated Results: All performance metrics were re-evaluated with these changes, particularly impacting the reported DT performance on medium and random datasets.

Metrics:

  • The reported DT Mean Reward and DT Success Rate (%) for medium and random datasets were significantly revised, reflecting the impact of the dynamic target_return.
  • The objective remained to measure the mean cumulative reward and success rates, but with an improved methodology for DT evaluation.

Key Files:

  • Modified: evaluate_and_visualize.py (critical update to DT evaluation logic), index.html, performance_results.csv.
  • Introduced: train_true_medium.py.
  • Updated binary models and videos for `cql_medium.d3` and `dt_medium.d3`.

Commit e9c7c2c: Update report to reflect improved normalized Decision Transformer metrics

This final analytical commit focused on optimizing the Decision Transformer's training hyperparameters and updating the reporting to showcase the best achievable performance with normalized metrics.

Experimentation History & Purpose:

  • To conduct hyperparameter tuning and apply best practices (like reward scaling) to maximize the performance of the Decision Transformer models.
  • To present the most optimized and robust metrics for DT, emphasizing the impact of normalization on learning stability and final performance.

Approach:

  • DT Optimizations (`train_improved_dt.py`): Introduced a new training script for DT incorporating:
    • Context Window Expansion: Increased context_size from 20 to 50, allowing the DT to process longer historical sequences.
    • Reward Scaling: Applied reward_scaler="standard" to normalize rewards, which is crucial for stabilizing Transformer training.
    • Adjusted batch_size=64 and learning_rate=1e-4 for improved optimization.
  • Streamlined Model Loading: Simplified model loading in evaluate_and_visualize.py using `d3rlpy.load_learnable()`.
  • Final Metric Update: All reported metrics, especially for DT, were updated to reflect the gains from these optimizations.

Metrics:

  • Reported the final, optimized Mean Cumulative Reward and Success Rate for Decision Transformer across all data qualities, with a specific focus on "improved normalized" metrics.

Key Files:

  • Modified: evaluate_and_visualize.py, index.html, performance_results.csv.
  • Introduced: train_improved_dt.py.
  • Updated DT model files and associated videos.
